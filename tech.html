CardiaHome

The technology behind it

Architecture, clinical logic, design, and quality — at a glance.

Architecture & stack

  • A single self-contained HTML file — no frameworks, no build step, no dependencies.
  • Vanilla JavaScript, inline CSS, and inline / code-generated SVG graphics and charts.
  • Runs fully offline — opens directly in any browser, even from a phone.
  • Design tokens drive the whole look, so the app can be re-skinned in one place.

Clinical engine

  • Green / Yellow / Red zone model, drawn from ACC/AHA heart-failure guidance.
  • Weight rules: +3 lb/day, +3 lb over two days, +5 lb/week, or +5 lb above baseline.
  • Runs on a rolling 7-day weight history; the worst of weight or symptoms sets the zone.
  • Each zone gives plain-language guidance and a one-tap line to the nurse.

Design & accessibility

  • Elderly-first: designed to the hardest case — low vision, shaky hands, low tech-comfort.
  • WCAG-AA contrast, large readable type, and 46–64px touch targets throughout.
  • Reduced-motion support; consistent SVG icons instead of platform-variable emoji.
  • Calm "premium-clinical" aesthetic — one accent colour, soft cards, generous space.

Quality & security

  • Passed independent design and security audits; all findings fixed.
  • No external calls and no injection points — graphics are built safely in code.
  • Every screen verified with automated screenshots during the build.
  • All patient data is fictional; clearly framed as a concept, not a medical device.
