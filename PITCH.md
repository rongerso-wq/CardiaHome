## The pitch

> Heart failure is the **#1 driver of hospital readmissions in older adults** — and most are preventable, signaled by a few pounds of fluid weight gain days before the crisis. CardiaHome is an **elderly-first app** that turns the daily weigh-in and symptom check into a simple **traffic-light zone** using real cardiology thresholds, then tells patients exactly what to do and **connects them to their nurse in one tap**. By catching trouble early and keeping patients tethered to their care team, it targets the **costliest, most avoidable event in cardiac care**.

---

## One-liner

CardiaHome catches heart-failure decline **before it lands a patient in the hospital** — a daily traffic-light check, built for the patients who need it most.

---

## The opportunity

- **~6.7M U.S. adults** live with heart failure today — projected to **~8.7M by 2030** and **11.4M by 2050**.
- HF costs the U.S. **~$30.7B/year**, projected to **~$69.8B by 2030**.
- **More than 1 in 5** HF patients is **readmitted within 30 days of discharge** — the costliest, most preventable event, and exactly what CardiaHome is built to head off.

_Source: Heart Failure Society of America, HF Stats 2024._

---

## Why it works (the evidence)

The feature set maps directly to what the literature shows reduces HF readmissions:
- **Early detection** — daily weight/symptom monitoring against ACC/AHA "zone" thresholds.
- **Clear action** — plain-language guidance the moment a patient enters the yellow/red zone.
- **Care-team connection** — one-tap call + messaging, the top engagement driver in HF self-care studies.
- **Built for the actual user** — accessible enough for a 75-year-old with low vision and shaky hands.
