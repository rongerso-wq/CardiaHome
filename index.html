Learn about your heart

A sudden gain of 3 lb in a day, 3 lb over two days, or 5 lb in a week often means your body is holding extra fluid — the earliest sign to act. Weigh each morning, after the toilet, before breakfast, in similar clothing.Thresholds follow the ACC/AHA Heart Failure guidelines and the Heart Failure “Zones” patient tool (Yancy CW et al., JACC 2013;62:e147–e239).

Salt makes your body hold water. Aim for under 2,000 mg of sodium a day: skip the salt shaker, rinse canned foods, and choose fresh over packaged when you can.

Call your nurse for: weight up 3+ lb, more swelling in your legs or feet, needing extra pillows to breathe, or a nagging cough. Call 911 for chest pain or severe breathlessness.

Your water pill (diuretic) clears extra fluid — take it in the morning so you’re not up at night. Never skip doses, even on days you feel well.
